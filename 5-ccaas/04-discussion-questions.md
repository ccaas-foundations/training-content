# CCaaS Reading Response

Read through the [AWS guide on Contact Center as a Service](https://aws.amazon.com/marketplace/solutions/business-applications/what-is/contact-center-as-a-service-ccaas), then discuss the following questions on your breakout rooms.

---

### Section 1 - What you already know

**1.** The guide lists ACD and IVR as core features of a CCaaS platform. In your own words, explain what each one does and how they work together during a single customer interaction. Don't use the article's definition - use what you learned this week.

**2.** The guide describes CRM integration as a feature that gives agents access to "customer information and history in real time." What is the specific mechanism that delivers that information to the agent? What is it called, and what triggers it?

**3.** The article draws a distinction between multichannel and omnichannel support. What is the difference? Why does it matter to a customer, and why does it matter to a developer building on one of these platforms?

**4.** The guide says CCaaS eliminates the need for "PBX systems and ACDs" as on-premise hardware. But ACDs still exist in CCaaS - what changed? What does an ACD look like in a cloud platform versus a physical server in a data center?

---

### Section 2 - Going further

**5.** The guide lists "Support for AI and Automation" as one of the key benefits of CCaaS. Based on what you've learned this week about the AI-native era of contact centers, what does this actually look like in practice? Name at least three specific things AI does in a modern contact center and where in the customer interaction each one occurs.

**6.** The article mentions skill-based routing as a feature. Think back to the ACD routing diagram. Skill-based routing is only one of the inputs into a routing decision - what are the others, and why would routing based on skills alone be insufficient?

**7.** The guide describes a phased implementation approach: assessment, vendor selection, transition, and training. Where does a developer fit into each of these phases? Be specific about what technical work happens at each stage.

**8.** The article describes a "subscription-based model" where businesses pay only for what they use. For a contact center with highly variable volume - a tax preparation company that gets 80% of its calls between January and April, for example - what does this mean operationally? How does this change the staffing and capacity planning conversation compared to an on-premise system?

---

### Section 3 - New territory

**9.** The guide mentions compliance standards: GDPR, HIPAA, and PCI DSS. You may not know all of these in detail, but think about what they have in common. Why is a contact center specifically a high-risk environment for data compliance? What kinds of customer data pass through a contact center interaction that would be subject to these regulations?

**10.** The article says CCaaS supports "disaster recovery and redundancy." For an on-premise contact center, what did disaster recovery actually require - physically and operationally? What does it mean for that same capability to be delivered as a cloud service?

**11.** The guide describes "change management" as a challenge during CCaaS adoption, specifically mentioning employee resistance and cultural adaptation. Think about this from the perspective of the agents and supervisors whose jobs are changing. What specifically changes about their day-to-day work when a contact center migrates to a cloud platform? Why might that create resistance even when a new system is objectively better?

**12.** The article frames CCaaS as a benefit to organizations. When could CCaaS create challenges for your organization? Think about it from the customer's perspective. What aspects of a well-implemented CCaaS platform directly improve the customer experience? And are there any ways a poorly implemented CCaaS could make the customer experience worse than what it replaced?
